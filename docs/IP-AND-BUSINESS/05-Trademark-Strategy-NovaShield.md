## TRADEMARK CLASSIFICATION FRAMEWORK

### Applicable International Classifications

**Class 42 — Scientific and Technological Services / Software:**
Covers computer software, software as a service (SaaS), technology platform services, and cloud-based software applications. The NovaShield platform's technical functionality — data collection, algorithmic scoring, database services — is properly classified in Class 42.

**Class 45 — Legal and Security Services / Social Services:**
Covers personal and social services for individuals, legal services, security services, and civic advocacy services. The NovaShield platform's purpose — facilitating legal rights information, connecting users with legal resources, enabling police accountability documentation — is properly classified in Class 45.

**Class 9 — Computer Software Products (goods):**
If the platform is ever distributed as a downloadable application, Class 9 covers downloadable mobile application software. Class 42 covers SaaS services; Class 9 covers downloadable goods.

**Class 35 — Business Services / Advertising:**
May be relevant if the Organization tier white-label service is positioned as a business services offering. Lower priority than Classes 42 and 45.

## MARK-BY-MARK ANALYSIS

### Priority 1 Marks — Immediate USPTO Filing Recommended

---

#### Mark 1: NOVASHIELD™

**Type:** Word mark (primary brand name)
**Classes:** 42, 45
**Strength Assessment:** STRONG — coined compound word

**Distinctiveness Analysis:**
"NovaShield" is a compound word combining "nova" (Latin: new; also a stellar astronomical term connoting brightness and power) with "shield" (protection, defense). Neither element in this combination describes software, law enforcement accountability platforms, or civic technology services. The combination is arbitrary with respect to the goods/services, satisfying the highest level of trademark distinctiveness (fanciful/coined or arbitrary marks).

The word "nova" appears in trademark registrations across diverse industries (Nova Healthcare, Nova Credit, Nova Entertainment) without saturation in the civic technology or police accountability space. The word "shield" appears in security, insurance, and healthcare contexts but with limited saturation in civic technology or police accountability. The combination "NovaShield" is not found in USPTO records as a registered or pending mark in Classes 42 or 45 based on initial clearance search.

**Recommended Filing:**
- USPTO Principal Register application (use-based if platform is in use at filing date; intent-to-use if not yet in commercial use)
- Classes 42 and 45 (dual-class filing)
- Description: "Computer software platforms and software as a service for police accountability monitoring, incident reporting, and community safety information; civic advocacy and legal information services for police accountability."
- Filing fee: ~$700 (two classes at TEAS Plus rate)

**Likelihood of Confusion Analysis:**
No identical or near-identical marks identified in Classes 42 or 45 in an initial search. Monitor during USPTO examination period for any office actions citing similar marks in adjacent classes.

#### Mark 2: OFFICER ACCOUNTABILITY SCORE™

**Type:** Word mark (product/feature name)
**Classes:** 42, 45
**Strength Assessment:** MODERATE — specific enough to be distinctive as a coined system name

**Distinctiveness Analysis:**
"Officer Accountability Score" is descriptive of the feature's purpose (scoring an officer's accountability). Descriptive marks require acquired distinctiveness (secondary meaning) for registration on the Principal Register. However, the specific combination — applied as the name of a proprietary algorithmic scoring system — may be registerable on the Principal Register as a product name/service mark rather than as a generic description of the function.

The USPTO allows registration of marks that are descriptive of a product when the mark has acquired distinctiveness through use or when the mark is used as a brand name rather than as a generic description. The filing strategy should position OFFICER ACCOUNTABILITY SCORE as the proper name of the NovaShield platform's proprietary scoring system, not as a description of scoring officer accountability generally.

**Recommended Filing:**
- USPTO Principal Register — with supporting declaration of secondary meaning if descriptiveness refusal is issued
- Alternatively, Supplemental Register filing initially with intent to seek Principal Register transfer after 5 years of use
- Classes 42 and 45
- Description: "Proprietary algorithmic scoring system for law enforcement officer accountability evaluation; software service for generating Officer Accountability Score metrics"

#### Mark 3: DISTRICT ACCOUNTABILITY SCORE™

**Strength Assessment:** MODERATE — same analysis as OFFICER ACCOUNTABILITY SCORE; more distinctive as a coined system name for geographic scoring

**Distinctiveness Analysis:**
"District Accountability Score" as applied to a proprietary system for computing composite accountability benchmarks for geographic law enforcement districts is registerable as a product/service mark. The specific combination is not found in USPTO records. The geographic scoring application to policing districts is novel enough to support distinctiveness.

**Recommended Filing:**

- Classes 42 and 45
- May be filed as a combined application with OFFICER ACCOUNTABILITY SCORE to reduce costs

#### Mark 4: "PROTECT YOUR RIGHTS. DEMAND ACCOUNTABILITY."™

**Type:** Service mark (tagline)
**Classes:** 42, 45
**Strength Assessment:** MODERATE-STRONG — distinctive tagline for the platform's mission

**Distinctiveness Analysis:**
Taglines are registerable as service marks when used consistently in commerce in connection with the associated service. "Protect Your Rights. Demand Accountability." is a distinctive expression of the NovaShield platform's core mission. The phrase "protect your rights" appears in some civil rights organizational communications but the specific combination with "Demand Accountability" as a unified dual-imperative tagline is distinctive. No registered mark with this specific combination was identified in USPTO records.

**Recommended Filing:**
- USPTO Principal Register — use-based application once the tagline appears consistently in platform marketing
- Classes 42 and 45
- Display the tagline with ™ designation in all platform marketing from launch date to establish use evidence

---

#### Mark 5: COMMUNITY SAFETY ALERT NETWORK™

**Type:** Word mark (product/system name)
**Classes:** 42, 45
**Strength Assessment:** MODERATE — descriptive but registerable as a system name

**Distinctiveness Analysis:**
"Community Safety Alert Network" describes a network of safety alerts for communities. As a generic description, it would be weak. As the specific branded name for the NovaShield platform's alert distribution system — and particularly given the novel inversion of the conventional alert paradigm (alerts *about* law enforcement rather than *from* law enforcement) — this mark has enough specificity to support registration as a product name.

**Recommended Filing:**
- Consider filing as a standard character mark and as a stylized mark if a distinctive logo treatment is developed
- Classes 42 and 45

### Priority 3 — Trade Dress and Proprietary Convention Protection

---

#### Mark 8: NS-YYYY-### Case Identifier Format

**Type:** Proprietary system convention / trade dress
**Protection Strategy:** Trade dress + trade secret (not trademark)

The NS-YYYY-### case numbering format is a proprietary identifier format that identifies NovaShield as the source of verified incident reports. This format is best protected as:
- Trade dress (the distinctive visual appearance of NovaShield case documents)
- Proprietary convention documented in platform materials
- Evidence of first use (timestamp of first NS-case-identifier assignment in platform database)

Traditional trademark registration of an identifier format is less standard, but trade dress protection for the recognizable case document format is available through consistent use and enforcement.

---

#### Mark 9: KNOW YOUR RIGHTS (in NovaShield context)

**Type:** Feature name
**Protection Strategy:** Common law + distinctive styling only

"Know Your Rights" is a widely used phrase in civil rights advocacy (used extensively by ACLU, NAACP, and others). It is not registerable as a trademark for civil rights information services due to descriptiveness and third-party prior use. Protect the NovaShield KYR module through distinctive visual styling and brand integration rather than trademark registration.

---

#### Mark 10: ACCOUNTABILITY TRACKER

**Type:** Feature name
**Protection Strategy:** Common law only

"Accountability Tracker" is too descriptive for trademark registration. No filing recommended. Use descriptively and protect through association with the NOVASHIELD™ brand.

## TRADEMARK MAINTENANCE AND ENFORCEMENT

### Monitoring Program
- Set up USPTO trademark monitoring for: "NovaShield," "accountability score" + "officer," "district accountability," and "community safety alert" + "law enforcement"
- Review monitoring alerts quarterly
- Watch for international applications in WIPO member countries if platform expands internationally

### Use Requirements
- USPTO registrations require a Declaration of Use between years 5–6 and renewal every 10 years
- Maintain clear ™ designation on all marks until registration issues, then use ® designation
- Document first use dates for all marks from platform launch

### Enforcement Strategy
- Cease and desist for clear infringement of NOVASHIELD™ as primary mark
- Monitor for "officer accountability score" or "district accountability score" being used by competitors to describe similar algorithmic outputs — the moment these are used by another party as brand names, enforce through USPTO opposition or cancellation proceedings
